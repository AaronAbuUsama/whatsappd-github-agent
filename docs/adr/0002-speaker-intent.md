# Speakers escalate evidence-backed Intents; the Brain decides

A Speaker may retrieve facts and converse in its own Surface, but it has no global authority. When conversation implies work or a cross-Surface consequence, it durably admits an immutable, evidence-backed Intent to the Brain's up-inbox and continues without waiting; the Brain alone chooses whether to act, where a result returns, or whether to stay silent.

## Considered options

- Command-shaped Intents were rejected because choosing an issue mutation, workflow, ontology write, or destination would leave the Speaker as the real decider and reduce the Brain to a router.
- Free-text context dumps were rejected because they duplicate raw conversation, lose exact provenance, and become stale before the Brain consumes them.
- Mutating an Intent as conversation clarifies was rejected because the original signal and evidence would disappear; clarification is another Intent for the stateful Brain to reconcile.
- Removing all read access from the Speaker was rejected because specific factual replies belong on the fast conversational path; authority, not observation, is the boundary.

## Consequences

The model selects non-empty observation identifiers from its bound Surface; trusted application code resolves them to immutable Conversation Events, binds the Surface, computes a content-addressed identity from the canonical evidence and interpretation, and atomically admits the Intent plus its inbox reference. Exact retries return the existing admission, admission and WhatsApp speech remain separate effects, the source Surface is provenance rather than a forced return address, and Speaker physically lacks GitHub mutation, Graph-write, Specialist-launch, and job-tracking tools.
